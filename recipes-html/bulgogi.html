Korean Bulgogi
Traditional marinated beef with Asian pear
Prep Time
4 hours
Cook Time
15 mins
Serves
4-6
Difficulty
Easy

üõí Ingredients

2 lbs thinly sliced ribeye or sirloin (1/8 inch thick)
1 large Asian pear, grated (about 1 cup)
1/2 cup soy sauce
3 tablespoons brown sugar
2 tablespoons sesame oil
1 tablespoon rice wine (mirin) or dry sherry
6 garlic cloves, minced
1 inch fresh ginger, grated
4 green onions, chopped
1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

üç¥ Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Box grater or food processor
  • Large skillet or grill pan
  • Tongs for flipping
  • Sharp knife
  • Cutting board

üìã Instructions

1
Grate the Asian pear using a box grater or food processor. In a large bowl, combine grated pear, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, rice wine, minced garlic, grated ginger, and half the green onions.
2
Add the sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring all pieces are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably 4 hours or overnight for best flavor.
3
Heat a large skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Remove beef from marinade, discarding excess liquid. Cook beef in batches for 2-3 minutes per side until caramelized.
4
Don't overcrowd the pan - work in batches if necessary. The beef should develop a nice brown color but remain tender inside.
5
Transfer to serving platter and garnish with remaining green onions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately with steamed rice and Korean side dishes.

üí° Notes & Tips

Asian Pear Benefits: The natural enzymes help tenderize the meat while adding subtle sweetness.

Meat Prep: Freeze beef for 30 minutes before slicing for easier thin cuts. Slice against the grain.

Substitutions: Can substitute Asian pear with grated apple or kiwi. For gluten-free, use tamari instead of soy sauce.

Serving: Traditionally served with lettuce wraps, kimchi, and steamed rice. Great for meal prep!
