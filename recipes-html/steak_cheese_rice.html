Steak, Cheese & Rice
One-skillet Mexican-inspired steak and rice with white queso
Prep Time
15 min
Cook Time
25 min
Serves
4
Difficulty
Easy

üõí Ingredients

1 cup long grain white rice
2 tbsp olive oil
1 small onion, chopped
1 tbsp minced garlic
2 cups chicken broth
8 oz tomato sauce
1/2 tsp each salt and pepper
1 tsp each cumin and dried cilantro
1 pound sirloin steak, cut up
2 tbsp butter
Montreal steak seasoning
Pancho's White Queso
Fresh cilantro for garnish
Flour tortillas for serving

üç¥ Equipment

  • Two large skillets
  • Lid for rice skillet
  • Wooden spoon or spatula
  • Cutting board and knife
  • Measuring cups and spoons

üìã Instructions

1
Rinse rice and add to a skillet over medium-high heat with olive oil. Toast rice, stirring frequently, until lightly golden.
2
Add chopped onion and minced garlic to rice. Stir well and cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
3
Add chicken broth, tomato sauce, and all seasonings. Stir well and bring to a boil. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook 20 minutes until rice is tender.
4
While rice cooks, heat butter in a second skillet over medium-high heat. Add cut steak, season with Montreal seasoning, and cook to desired doneness.
5
Top cooked rice with steak and drizzle with white queso. Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve in bowls or with flour tortillas.
