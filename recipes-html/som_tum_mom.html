üìã Instructions

1
Soak julienned green papaya in ice water for 10-15 minutes to crisp up and remove any bitterness. Drain well before using.
2
In mortar and pestle, pound toasted peanuts roughly until broken into smaller pieces. Set aside a portion for garnish.
3
In small bowl, melt palm sugar with 2 tablespoons water until completely dissolved. This ensures even sweetness distribution.
4
In mortar, pound green beans, chili, and a pinch of salt roughly. Add soaked dried shrimp and crushed peanuts, pound gently to combine.
5
Add drained papaya, cherry tomatoes, melted palm sugar mixture, lime juice, and fish sauce. Pound and toss thoroughly until all ingredients are well combined and papaya is slightly softened but still crispy.

üí° Chef's Notes & Tips

Ice Water Technique: The ice water soak makes papaya extra crispy and removes any bitterness - a signature step in Mom's version.

Toasted Peanuts: Pre-toasted peanuts add wonderful texture and deeper nutty flavor compared to raw peanuts.

Bird's Eye Chili: Provides authentic Thai heat - start with 1 and add more to taste. Much hotter than regular chilies!

Palm Sugar Method: Melting palm sugar with water creates better distribution and prevents clumping.

Layered Technique: Building flavors gradually in the mortar creates the best balance.
