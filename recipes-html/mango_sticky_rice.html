Sticky Rice in Rice Cooker
Thai Mango Sticky Rice - Easy Rice Cooker Method

For the Coconut Sauce:

  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2-3 tbsp liquid palm sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
2 cups sticky rice (glutinous rice)
Water for soaking and cooking
2-3 ripe mangoes, sliced
Toasted peanuts for garnish
Extra coconut milk for serving

üç¥ Equipment

  • Rice cooker
  • Fine mesh strainer
  • Measuring cups
  • Mixing bowl
  • Small saucepan
  • Serving plates

üìã Instructions

1
Rinse 2 cups of sticky rice in cold water until water runs clear. Soak rice for 30 minutes in enough water to cover.
2
Add water until rice is completely submerged (about 7/8 new level). Continue soaking for additional 40 minutes total.
3
Drain water until it just barely covers the rice, maintaining a 1:1 ratio of rice to water.
4
Transfer to rice cooker and use white rice setting to cook. Meanwhile, prepare coconut sauce by heating coconut milk, palm sugar, and salt in saucepan until sugar dissolves.
5
Let rice rest in cooker for 5 minutes, then fluff gently. Serve with sliced mango, drizzle with coconut sauce, and garnish with toasted peanuts.

üí° Chef's Notes & Tips

Rice Quality: Use only glutinous rice (sticky rice) - regular rice won't work for this dish.

Soaking is Key: Don't skip the soaking time - it's essential for proper texture and cooking.

Mango Selection: Choose ripe but firm mangoes like Tommy Atkins or Kent varieties.

Storage: Best served fresh, but leftovers keep 2 days refrigerated.

üõí Ingredients

For the Coconut Sauce:

  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 3-4 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
2 cups glutinous rice
Water for overnight soaking
2-3 ripe yellow mangoes
Toasted mung beans (optional)
Extra coconut milk for serving

üç¥ Equipment

  • Large steamer basket
  • Large pot with tight-fitting lid
  • Cheesecloth or banana leaves
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Small saucepan
  • Fine mesh strainer

üìã Instructions

1
Soak glutinous rice overnight (8+ hours) in enough water to cover by 2 inches. Drain and rinse before cooking.
2
Line steamer basket with cheesecloth. Add soaked rice and steam over boiling water for 25-30 minutes until tender and translucent.
3
Meanwhile, prepare coconut sauce: heat coconut milk with sugar and salt over low heat. Simmer until sugar dissolves completely. Remove from heat and let cool.
4
Transfer hot steamed rice to bowl. Pour coconut mixture over rice, mix well. Cover and let rest for 1 hour - this "MOON" process allows rice to absorb all the coconut milk.
5
Slice mangoes and arrange with sticky rice. Garnish with toasted mung beans and serve with extra coconut milk on the side.

üí° Chef's Notes & Tips

Overnight Soaking: This is non-negotiable for authentic texture - plan ahead!

The "MOON" Process: Thai technique where rice absorbs coconut milk completely - patience is key.

Steaming Tips: Keep water at gentle boil, don't let it touch the rice. Check water level regularly.

Serving: Best at room temperature. Traditional presentation uses banana leaves.

üáπüá≠ Cultural Notes

About Khao Niao Mamuang: This beloved Thai dessert represents the perfect balance of sweet, salty, and creamy flavors. Traditionally served during mango season (March-June), it's considered one of Thailand's national desserts. The dish originated in Central Thailand and has been enjoyed for centuries as both a street food and restaurant delicacy.
