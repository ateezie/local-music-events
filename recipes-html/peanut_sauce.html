Mom's Peanut Sauce
A classic Asian-style peanut sauce perfect for dipping, drizzling, or dressing
Prep Time
5 min
Cook Time
None
Serves
4-6
Difficulty
Easy

üõí Ingredients

Perfect for serving with:

  • Spring rolls or summer rolls
  • Grilled chicken satay
  • Cold noodle salads
  • Fresh vegetables as dip
  • Rice bowls or grain salads
1/3 cup peanut butter (creamy works best)
2 tablespoons rice vinegar
2 tablespoons soy sauce
2 tablespoons maple syrup
1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
2 cloves garlic, minced
2-3 tablespoons warm water

üç¥ Equipment

  • Medium mixing bowl
  • Whisk or fork
  • Garlic press or knife for mincing
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Small serving bowl or jar

üìã Instructions

1
In a medium bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and maple syrup until well combined and smooth.
2
Add the minced garlic and toasted sesame oil to the mixture, whisking until fully incorporated.
3
Gradually whisk in 2-3 tablespoons of warm water, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired consistency. The sauce should be smooth and pourable but not too thin.
4
Taste and adjust seasonings as needed. Add more soy sauce for saltiness, maple syrup for sweetness, or rice vinegar for tang.
5
Transfer to serving bowl or storage container. Use immediately or store covered in refrigerator for up to one week. Thin with additional warm water if needed before serving.

üí° Chef's Notes & Tips

Consistency Tips: The sauce should coat the back of a spoon but still drizzle easily. If too thick, add water 1 teaspoon at a time.

Flavor Variations: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes for heat, fresh ginger for zing, or lime juice for extra brightness.

Make-Ahead: This sauce actually improves in flavor after sitting. Make it a few hours ahead for best results.

Storage: The sauce will thicken in the refrigerator - just whisk in a little warm water to restore consistency.
