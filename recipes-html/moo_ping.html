üìã Instructions

1

2
Freeze pork for 15-30 minutes to make slicing easier. This helps achieve clean, even cuts.
3
Slice pork against the grain into ¬æ" wide, ‚Öõ" thick strips. Keeping uniform thickness ensures even cooking.
4
Combine pork with marinade in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate for 4-8 hours, or overnight for best flavor.
5
Thread pork onto soaked wooden skewers tightly, weaving through each piece to secure. Leave space at ends for handling.
6
Grill skewers over medium-high heat. Brush frequently with coconut milk and flip often. Cook for 8-10 minutes total until caramelized and cooked through.
7
For dipping sauce: Mix fish sauce, sugar, garlic, chilies, and vinegar. Add toasted rice powder at the end and mix well.
8
Serve hot with lime wedges, fresh cilantro, and Nam Jim Jeaw dipping sauce.

üí° Chef's Notes & Tips

Authentic Flavor: Moo Ping is a beloved Thai street food. The key is the balance of sweet, salty, and savory in the marinade.

Slicing Tip: Freezing the pork briefly makes it much easier to slice uniformly thin.

Coconut Milk: Don't skip the coconut milk basting - it prevents drying and adds authentic flavor.

Rice Powder: Add to dipping sauce just before serving to maintain crunch. Can substitute with crushed toasted rice.

Serving: Traditionally served with sticky rice and Som Tam (papaya salad).
