About Holy Basil (Ka Prao):

Thai holy basil has a spicier, more intense flavor than sweet basil. Look for it at Asian markets. Thai basil is the best substitute if unavailable.

3-5 Thai chilies, roughly crushed
3-4 garlic cloves, roughly crushed
1 cup Thai holy basil leaves (ka prao)
200g ground pork, chicken, or beef
2 tablespoons vegetable oil
2 tablespoons fish sauce
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 teaspoon sugar
2 eggs for frying
Cooked jasmine rice for serving

üç¥ Equipment

  • Wok or Large Skillet
  • Mortar and Pestle (preferred)
  • Small Frying Pan for Eggs
  • Wooden Spoon or Wok Spatula
  • Knife and Cutting Board

üìã Instructions

1
Using mortar and pestle, roughly crush chilies and garlic together. Keep pieces chunky, not paste-like. Heat oil in wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking.
2
Add crushed chilies and garlic to hot oil. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant and sizzling. Be careful not to burn.
3
Add ground meat and break apart with spatula. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes over high heat until meat is cooked through and slightly crispy around edges.
4
Add fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir well to combine and cook for 1 minute to let flavors meld and sauce reduce slightly.
5
Add holy basil leaves and toss quickly for 30 seconds until wilted. Remove from heat immediately. Meanwhile, fry eggs in separate pan until crispy-edged. Serve over rice with fried egg on top.

üí° Chef's Notes & Tips

High Heat is Key: Use the highest heat possible for authentic "wok hei" (breath of the wok) flavor that makes Thai stir-fries special.

Crispy Egg: The fried egg should have golden, crispy, lacy edges. This textural contrast is essential to the dish.

Basil Timing: Add basil at the very end and remove from heat immediately to preserve its aromatic oils.

Serving Style: Traditionally served with extra chilies and fish sauce on the side for customization.

üî• Heat Level Guide

Mild (1-2 chilies): Perfect for beginners or those sensitive to heat.

Medium (3-4 chilies): Traditional Bangkok street food level.

Hot (5-7 chilies): Southern Thai style, very spicy.

Thai Hot (8+ chilies): Only for serious chili lovers!

ü•¨ Basil Guide

1
Holy Basil (Ka Prao): Best choice. Spicy, peppery flavor with purple-tinged stems. Found at Thai/Asian markets.
2
Thai Sweet Basil: Good substitute. Sweeter, more anise-like flavor. More commonly available.
3
Italian Basil: Last resort. Much milder flavor. Use double the amount and add at very end.
4
Storage Tip: Keep basil stems in water like flowers. Don't refrigerate - it will turn black.

üç≥ Perfect Fried Egg

Oil Temperature: Heat oil until shimmering and very hot before adding egg.

Technique: Crack egg from low height. Oil should bubble and sizzle around whites immediately.

Result: Whites should be crispy and lacy around edges, yolk still runny.

Safety: Be careful of oil splatter when adding egg to hot oil.

Cultural Notes:

Pad Ka Prao is Thailand's most popular one-dish meal, served at virtually every street stall and restaurant. It's commonly eaten for lunch and is considered comfort food.

The dish originated in central Thailand but variations exist throughout the country, with southern versions being spicier.
