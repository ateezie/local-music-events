Traditional Thai Stir-Fried Rice Noodles - Product of Thailand
Prep Time
20 min
Cook Time
8-10 min
Serves
2-3
Difficulty
Medium

üõí Ingredients

Pad Thai Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp Coconut Sugar (or Palm Sugar)
  • 3 tbsp Fish Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Tamarind Juice
  • 1 tsp Chili Sauce (adjust to taste)
200g Rice Noodles (Pad Thai width)
2 Large Eggs
100g Firm Tofu, cubed
1 cup Fresh Bean Sprouts
3-4 Chinese Chives, cut 2-inch pieces
2 tbsp Dried Shrimp (optional)
2 tbsp Pickled Radish, chopped
¬º cup Crushed Peanuts
1 tsp Dried Chili Powder
1 Lime, cut into wedges
Vegetable Oil for cooking
Banana Flower for garnish (optional)

üç¥ Equipment

  • Large Wok or Heavy-bottomed Skillet
  • Wooden Spatula or Wok Turner
  • Large Bowl for soaking noodles
  • Small Bowl for mixing sauce
  • Knife and Cutting Board
  • Strainer

üìã Instructions

1
Soak rice noodles in warm water until soft and pliable, about 30 minutes. Drain well. In a small bowl, whisk together all sauce ingredients until sugar dissolves completely. Set aside.
2
Heat 2 tablespoons oil in large wok or skillet over high heat. Add cubed tofu and stir-fry until golden brown on all sides, about 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside on plate.
3
In same wok, crack eggs and scramble quickly. Stir fry in hot oil until eggs are cooked through but still slightly wet. Push eggs to one side of wok.
4
Add drained rice noodles to wok. Pour Pad Thai sauce over noodles. Using wooden spatula, toss everything together constantly to prevent sticking. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until noodles are heated through and sauce is absorbed.
5
Add bean sprouts, Chinese chives, dried shrimp (if using), pickled radish, and cooked tofu. Continue stir-frying for another 2 minutes until vegetables are slightly wilted but still crisp.
6
Remove from heat immediately. Transfer to serving plates. Garnish with crushed peanuts, chili powder, and lime wedges. Serve with banana flower if available. Enjoy while hot!

üí° Thai Cooking Tips

Noodle Texture: Don't over-soak the noodles - they should be pliable but still have some bite. They'll finish cooking in the wok.

Wok Hei: Cook over high heat and keep ingredients moving constantly for that authentic "breath of the wok" flavor that makes restaurant-style Pad Thai.

Tamarind Substitute: If tamarind juice unavailable, mix 1 tbsp lime juice + 1 tbsp rice vinegar as substitute.

Vegetarian Version: Omit dried shrimp and replace fish sauce with soy sauce or vegetarian mushroom sauce.

üáπüá≠ Cultural Background

National Identity: Pad Thai was promoted as Thailand's national dish in the 1930s by Prime Minister Plaek Phibunsongkhram as part of a campaign to promote Thai nationalism and unity.

Street Food Heritage: Originally sold by street vendors throughout Thailand, Pad Thai represents the essence of Thai street food culture - quick, flavorful, and made fresh to order.

Balance of Flavors: True to Thai cuisine principles, Pad Thai balances sweet (coconut sugar), sour (tamarind), salty (fish sauce), and spicy (chili) flavors in perfect harmony.

Regional Variations: While this recipe represents the central Thai style, different regions of Thailand have their own variations and preferred ingredients.

ü•¢ Serving Traditions

Condiment Station: In Thailand, Pad Thai is often served with small bowls of sugar, chili flakes, fish sauce, and lime for individual customization.

Banana Leaf: Street vendors traditionally wrap Pad Thai in banana leaves, which adds a subtle aroma and keeps the dish warm.

Fresh Garnishes: Bean sprouts, lime wedges, and crushed peanuts are essential fresh garnishes that add texture and flavor contrast.

üîÑ Popular Variations

1
Pad Thai Gai: Add sliced chicken breast during step 4, cooking until done before adding noodles.
2
Pad Thai Goong: Include medium shrimp, cooking until pink before adding noodles.
3
Pad Thai Jay (Vegetarian): Omit fish sauce and dried shrimp. Use soy sauce and add extra vegetables like carrots or cabbage.
4
Pad Thai Woon Sen: Replace rice noodles with glass noodles (bean thread noodles) for a different texture.
5
Pad Thai Phak Ruam: Add mixed vegetables like bell peppers, snap peas, or baby corn for extra nutrition and crunch.

üå∂Ô∏è Authentic Thai Tips

High Heat is Key: Thai cooking relies on extremely high heat. If your stove doesn't get hot enough, cook in smaller batches to maintain temperature.

Ingredient Quality: Use authentic tamarind paste if possible - it makes a significant difference in flavor compared to substitutes.

Timing: Pad Thai should be served immediately after cooking. The noodles continue to absorb sauce and can become mushy if left sitting.
